Member igl::hausdorff (const Eigen::MatrixBase< DerivedVA > &VA, const Eigen::MatrixBase< DerivedFA > &FA, const Eigen::MatrixBase< DerivedVB > &VB, const Eigen::MatrixBase< DerivedFB > &FB, Scalar &d)
This is only computing max(min(va,B),min(vb,A)). This is better than max(min(va,Vb),min(vb,Va)). This (at least) is missing "edge-edge" cases like the distance between the two different triangulations of a non-planar quad in 3D. Even simpler, consider the Hausdorff distance between the non-convex, block letter V polygon (with 7 vertices) in 2D and its convex hull. The Hausdorff distance is defined by the midpoint in the middle of the segment across the concavity and some non-vertex point on the edge of the V. Known issue: due to the issue above, this also means that unreferenced vertices can give unexpected results. Therefore, we assume the inputs have no unreferenced vertices.
